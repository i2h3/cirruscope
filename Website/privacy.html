Privacy Policy

Last updated July 16, 2026

Cirruscope does not collect any data. Not usage analytics, not crash reports, not your files, nothing — full stop.

What Cirruscope does on your Mac

Cirruscope connects directly to the Nextcloud server you choose. Your sign-in session is stored securely in the macOS Keychain, on your device only. Cirruscope contains no analytics, tracking, or crash-reporting code, and it never sends any data to us or anyone else.

Your Nextcloud server

The files, messages, and other content you access through Cirruscope live on the Nextcloud server you connect to — your own server, or one run by a provider you chose. That server's own privacy policy governs how it handles your data; this policy only covers the Cirruscope app itself.

Third parties

Cirruscope doesn't use analytics providers, advertising networks, or third-party SDKs of any kind.

Children's privacy

Cirruscope is not directed at children and, consistent with everything above, doesn't knowingly collect information from anyone, of any age.

Changes to this policy

If this policy ever changes, we'll update this page and revise the date at the top.

See for yourself

Cirruscope is open source. If you'd like to verify any of this, the full source code is on GitHub.

Contact

Questions about this policy? Email contact@cirruscope.app.
