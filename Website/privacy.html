The short version

Cirruscope does not collect, store, sell, or share any personal data. No analytics, no telemetry, no tracking, no ads, and no account with us. Everything happens directly between your Mac and the Nextcloud server you choose.†

† This covers the Cirruscope app and this website. The Nextcloud server you connect to still stores and processes your data under the privacy terms of whoever operates it — see Your Nextcloud server.

Overview

Cirruscope is a free, open-source macOS application, built and maintained by an independent developer. It displays your Nextcloud instance inside a native Mac window and adds macOS conveniences such as window management, notifications, and keyboard shortcuts.

Because Cirruscope is essentially a native container around the Nextcloud web interface, the application itself is not a service and does not gather information about you. This policy explains exactly what that means in practice.

Cirruscope is an independent project and is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or sponsored by Nextcloud GmbH. This policy covers only the Cirruscope application and this website — not your Nextcloud server or Nextcloud GmbH’s own services.

What Cirruscope doesn’t collect

Cirruscope contains no analytics, telemetry, advertising, or third-party tracking of any kind. Specifically, the developer does not collect:

do_not_disturb_onPersonal information (name, email, address, phone)
do_not_disturb_onUsage analytics, behavioral data, or crash telemetry
do_not_disturb_onYour files, folders, messages, or their contents
do_not_disturb_onYour Nextcloud credentials or session tokens
do_not_disturb_onAdvertising or device identifiers
verified

On the Mac App Store, Cirruscope’s privacy label is declared as “Data Not Collected.”

Information stored on your device

To keep you signed in and remember your preferences, Cirruscope stores some data locally on your Mac, within your user account:

Session data & cookies set by your Nextcloud server, managed by macOS/WebKit like any website session.
Login credentials, when you choose to save them, stored securely in the macOS Keychain.
App preferences such as your server list, window layout, and appearance settings.
Web cache the browser engine keeps to load your Nextcloud faster.

This data never leaves your Mac and is never sent to the developer. You can clear it at any time by signing out, clearing the app’s data in its settings, or removing the app.

Your Nextcloud server

When you sign in, Cirruscope connects directly to the Nextcloud server address you provide. All of your files, messages, and account activity are handled by that server. Anything you do in Cirruscope is governed by the privacy policy and terms of whoever operates that Nextcloud instance — which may be you, your employer, or a hosting provider.

The developer of Cirruscope operates no servers, and has no access to your Nextcloud data, credentials, or activity.

Network connections

Cirruscope makes network requests only for these purposes:

Your Nextcloud server(s) — the addresses you enter, to load and sync your content.
App updates are delivered through the Mac App Store by Apple. Cirruscope does not phone home or contact any developer-run server.

This website

This website is a static site hosted on GitHub Pages. It sets no cookies, runs no analytics or tracking, and loads no third-party resources — the fonts, icon, and images are all served from the site itself, so your browser never contacts an outside service. As with any web host, GitHub may keep standard server logs (such as IP addresses) to deliver the site.

We do not use this website to identify or track you.

Children’s privacy

Cirruscope is a general-purpose tool and is not directed at children. It collects no personal information from anyone, including children.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, the updated version will be posted on this page with a new “last updated” date. Because the project is open source, the full history of changes is also available in the repository.
